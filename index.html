Gardening School

As schools become more awrare of the benefits of experiential learning, school gardens have been transformed in outdoor classrooms, enabling students to discover science and the natural environment through fun, engaging and hands-on gardening activities.

Indeed, students are encouraged to get involved with their school gardens right from the start, by helping out with the planning and designing of their gardens, and maintaining their plots on a regular basis.Both teachers anf students are eqipped with basic plant care skills such as watering, plantingm fertilising and pruning. Gardening starter kits are also available, to help them along their experiential journey.

Learning gardens have proven to be popular among students. Xiao Zhen, from Concorf Primary enjoys her gardening sessions."I love to do gardening in school. I love plants and I like to see plants growing higher and higher, frim a seed to a tall, tall tree! When I do gardening, I feel special, as I enjoy taking care of the plants, and helping in watering and pulling out weeds."
